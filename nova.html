Nova

A "Nova" is a sudden and temporary increase in the brightness of a star system. In Latin, Nova Stella translates to "New Star." In ancient times, when people observed such explosions in the sky, they mistakenly thought a brand-new star had been born, which is how it got its name. However, it is actually a massive nuclear explosion occurring on the surface of an existing star.

  • One star must be a White Dwarf (the dense core remaining from a dead star).
  • The other star is usually a normal star or a Red Giant.
The intense gravity of the White Dwarf pulls hydrogen gas from its companion star. This gas accumulates on the surface of the White Dwarf and becomes extremely hot. Once the temperature and pressure reach a critical point, a rapid Nuclear Fusion process ignites on the surface, resulting in a massive explosion.

  • Brightness: The star’s brightness can increase by thousands of times its normal state.
  • Duration: This peak brightness can last for several days or weeks before gradually fading away.
  • Recurrence: Unlike a Supernova, the star is not completely destroyed during a Nova. Therefore, the same star can undergo another Nova explosion years later (known as Recurrent Novae).

Feature Nova Supernova
Nature An explosion on the surface of the star. The entire star explodes and is destroyed.
Outcome The star survives. The star is destroyed or leaves behind a Black Hole/Neutron Star.
Brightness Very bright, but not as much as a Supernova. Can be brighter than an entire galaxy.

  1. Classical Novae: Extremely bright explosions that have been observed only once in a specific system.
  2. Recurrent Novae: Stars that explode repeatedly, typically every 10 to 100 years.
  3. Dwarf Novae: Smaller outbursts caused by instabilities in the "Accretion Disk" (the disk of gas surrounding the White Dwarf).

Nova explosions contribute significantly to the distribution of chemical elements in the universe (such as Lithium). Furthermore, scientists study these events to measure cosmic distances and to better understand the evolution of stars.
