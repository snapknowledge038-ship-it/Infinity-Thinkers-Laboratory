Nova

What is a Nova?

A "Nova" is a sudden and temporary increase in the brightness of a star system. In Latin, "Nova Stella" means "New Star." While ancient observers often mistook this for the birth of a new star, it is actually a massive nuclear explosion occurring on the surface of an existing star.

How it Occurs

This typically happens in binary star systems (systems with two stars):
  • One star must be a White Dwarf (a dense, collapsed star).
  • The other is usually a normal star or a Red Giant.
The extreme gravity of the White Dwarf pulls hydrogen gas from its companion star. As this gas accumulates on the surface and the temperature rises, it triggers a massive nuclear explosion.

Comparison: Nova vs Supernova

Feature Nova Supernova
Nature An explosion on the star's surface. A cataclysmic explosion that destroys the entire star.
Outcome The star survives (can explode again). Leaves behind a Black Hole or a Neutron Star.
Brightness Very bright. Can outshine an entire galaxy.

Key Characteristics

  • Brightness: The star's luminosity can increase by thousands of times its normal state.
  • Duration: The peak brightness can last for several days or weeks before fading.
  • Recurrence: Since the star is not destroyed, a nova explosion can happen again after many years.
