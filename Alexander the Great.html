ALEXANDER THE GREAT

The King of Macedon

Alexander III of Macedon was a king of the ancient Greek kingdom of Macedon. Born in 356 BC, he was tutored by the famous philosopher Aristotle and became one of history's most successful military commanders.

Legendary Achievements

  • Undefeated in Battle: Alexander spent 12 years on campaign and never lost a single battle.
  • Massive Empire: By the age of 30, he had created one of the largest empires in history, stretching from Greece to northwestern India.
  • Cultural Fusion: He spread Greek culture (Hellenism) across the east, which influenced art and science for centuries.

The Mystery of His Death

At the young age of 32, Alexander died in Babylon. The cause remains a mystery—some say it was fever or malaria, while others believe he was poisoned by his rivals.
