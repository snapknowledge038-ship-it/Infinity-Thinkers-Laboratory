The Moon

What is the Moon?

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. It is the fifth largest natural satellite in the solar system and is located approximately 384,400 kilometers away from Earth.

Physical Characteristics

  • Atmosphere: The Moon has no significant atmosphere. Because of this, there is no medium for sound to travel, making the Moon completely silent.
  • Temperature: Temperatures can rise to 127°C during the day and drop to an extremely cold -173°C at night.
  • Gravity: The Moon's gravity is about 1/6th of Earth's. (e.g., If you weigh 60kg on Earth, you would feel like you weigh only 10kg on the Moon).

Motion and Appearance

  • Synchronous Rotation: The Moon takes the same amount of time to rotate on its axis as it does to orbit Earth (27.3 days). This is why we always see only one side of the Moon.
  • Lunar Phases: As the Moon orbits Earth, the amount of sunlight reflecting off its surface changes, creating phases from New Moon to Full Moon.

Surface Features

  1. Lunar Highlands: Light-colored, mountainous regions covered with numerous craters.
  2. Maria: Dark, flat plains formed by ancient volcanic eruptions.

Importance of the Moon

  • Tides: The Moon's gravitational pull causes the rise and fall of ocean sea levels, known as tides.
  • Earth's Tilt: The Moon helps stabilize Earth's 23.5-degree axial tilt. This stability is what gives us our predictable seasons and climate.

Lunar Exploration

  • Apollo 11 (1969): Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to step foot on the Moon.
  • Modern Day: Currently, countries like the USA (Artemis), India (Chandrayaan), and China are launching missions to search for water at the Moon's South Pole.
