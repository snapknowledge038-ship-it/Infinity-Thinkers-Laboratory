NAPOLEON BONAPARTE

The Emperor of the French

Born in Corsica in 1769, Napoleon Bonaparte rose to prominence during the French Revolution. He crowned himself Emperor in 1804 and dominated European affairs for over a decade.

Military Brilliance

  • The Napoleonic Wars: He led a series of successful campaigns against various coalitions of European nations, showcasing unmatched tactical genius.
  • The Napoleonic Code: He established a legal system that heavily influenced the civil law of many countries worldwide.
  • Master of Artillery: His revolutionary use of mobile artillery was a key factor in his many battlefield victories.

The Final Defeat

After a disastrous invasion of Russia, Napoleon's power began to fade. His final defeat came at the Battle of Waterloo in 1815, after which he was exiled to the remote island of Saint Helena.
