Meteor

1. What is a Meteor?

Small rocky or metallic objects that enter the Earth's atmosphere from space are called meteors. Due to the friction caused by traveling at high speeds through the air, they burn up and create a bright streak of light. These are commonly known as "Shooting Stars."

2. The Three Stages

  • Meteoroid: Small fragments of rock or debris traveling through space.
  • Meteor: The streak of light seen when a meteoroid enters the atmosphere and burns up.
  • Meteorite: Fragments that do not burn up completely in the atmosphere and land on Earth's surface.

3. Types of Meteorites

  1. Stony Meteorites: The most common type, composed primarily of silicate minerals.
  2. Iron Meteorites: A very heavy type consisting mainly of iron and nickel.
  3. Stony-Iron Meteorites: A rare variety containing a mixture of both metallic and rocky material.

4. Meteor Showers

Meteor showers occur when Earth passes through a trail of dust and debris left behind by a comet. During this time, many meteors can be seen falling from the same point in the sky (e.g., Perseids and Geminids showers).

5. Quick Facts Summary

Feature Description
Speed Range from 11 to 72 kilometers per second

Size Range from tiny grains of sand to massive boulders
