WORLD WAR II

1939 — 1945 • Total Global Warfare

The Conflict That Changed Everything

World War II was the deadliest and most widespread war in history, involving more than 100 million people from over 30 countries. It was a "Total War," where nations threw their entire economic, industrial, and scientific capabilities into the war effort, blurring the lines between civilian and military resources.

The Major Alliances

The Allies

Fighting to stop the expansion of tyranny.

  • United Kingdom: Led by Winston Churchill.
  • Soviet Union (USSR): Led by Joseph Stalin.
  • United States: Led by FDR and later Truman.
  • China & France: Major resistance forces.

The Axis Powers

Seeking to establish a new world order.

  • Nazi Germany: Led by Adolf Hitler.
  • Imperial Japan: Led by Emperor Hirohito.
  • Fascist Italy: Led by Benito Mussolini.

Turning Points

1941 - Pearl Harbor: Japan attacks the US naval base in Hawaii, bringing the United States into the war.
1942 - Battle of Stalingrad: The turning point on the Eastern Front; the Soviet Union begins pushing the Germans back.
1944 - D-Day: The largest seaborne invasion in history as Allied forces land on the beaches of Normandy, France.
1945 - Hiroshima & Nagasaki: The US drops atomic bombs on Japan, leading to their unconditional surrender.

Technological & Human Impact

WWII saw incredible leaps in technology, including Jet Engines, Radar, and the Atomic Bomb. However, it also witnessed the darkest moments of humanity, most notably the Holocaust—the systematic genocide of six million Jews by the Nazi regime.

  • The Blitz: Heavy aerial bombing of cities like London.
  • The Pacific Front: Brutal island-hopping campaigns across the ocean.
  • Resistance Movements: Underground fighters across Europe and Asia.

A New World Order

The war ended with the fall of Berlin and the surrender of Japan in August 1945. The aftermath saw the creation of the United Nations to prevent future conflicts and the beginning of the Cold War between the two remaining superpowers: the USA and the USSR.
