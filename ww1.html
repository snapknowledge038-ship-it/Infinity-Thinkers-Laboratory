WORLD WAR I

1914 — 1918 • The War to End All Wars

Overview

World War I, also known as the Great War, was a global conflict that redefined the borders of the world and changed the course of human history forever. Spanning four years, it involved the mobilization of over 70 million military personnel and resulted in the collapse of four major empires.

The Spark: Origins

While tensions had been simmering for decades due to M.A.I.N. (Militarism, Alliances, Imperialism, and Nationalism), the immediate cause was the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary on June 28, 1914, in Sarajevo.

The Allies

Primarily France, Great Britain, Russia, Italy, and the United States.

The Central Powers

Primarily Germany, Austria-Hungary, and the Ottoman Empire.

Life in the Trenches

The Western Front was defined by Trench Warfare. Millions of soldiers lived in muddy, rat-infested ditches stretching from the Swiss border to the North Sea. The area between opposing trenches was a lethal wasteland known as "No Man's Land."

  • The Stalemate: Defensive technology (machine guns) was superior to offensive tactics, leading to years of deadlock.
  • Psychological Toll: Soldiers faced constant shelling, leading to a condition then known as "Shell Shock."
  • Conditions: Trench foot, lice, and disease were often as deadly as the enemy.

Modern Warfare Technologies

WWI saw the birth of industrial-scale killing through new inventions:

  • Poison Gas: Chlorine and Mustard gas caused agonizing injuries and terror.
  • War Birds: For the first time, airplanes were used for reconnaissance and "dogfights."
  • Iron Giants: The British introduced the Tank to break through barbed wire and trenches.
  • U-Boats: German submarines targeted merchant ships, eventually bringing the US into the war.

Major Turning Points

  • 1916 - Battle of the Somme: One of the deadliest battles in history, with over 1 million casualties.
  • 1917 - Russian Revolution: Internal collapse forced Russia to exit the war, shifting balance to the East.
  • 1917 - US Entry: The United States joined the Allies, bringing fresh troops and massive industrial power.

The Aftermath

The war ended with an armistice on November 11, 1918. In 1919, the Treaty of Versailles was signed, forcing Germany to accept full responsibility for the war. This created economic instability and resentment that many historians believe led directly to the rise of the Nazis and World War II.
