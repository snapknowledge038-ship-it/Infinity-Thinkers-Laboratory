CLEOPATRA VII

The Last Pharaoh

Cleopatra VII Philopator was the last active ruler of the Ptolemaic Kingdom of Egypt. She was not just a queen of beauty, but a brilliant diplomat, naval commander, and polyglot who spoke over nine languages.

Political Strategy

  • Alliance with Caesar: She formed a powerful political and personal relationship with Julius Caesar to secure her throne.
  • The Roman Connection: After Caesar's death, she aligned with Mark Antony, challenging the rising power of Octavian (Augustus).
  • Economic Prosperity: Under her reign, Egypt remained the wealthiest nation in the Mediterranean world.

The End of an Era

Following her defeat at the Battle of Actium, Cleopatra chose to end her life in 30 BC. Legend says she died from the bite of an asp (Egyptian cobra), marking the end of the Pharaohs and the beginning of Egypt as a Roman province.
