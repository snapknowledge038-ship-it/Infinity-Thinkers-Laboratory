ADOLF HITLER

Rise to Power

Born in Austria in 1889, Adolf Hitler rose to become the leader of the Nazi Party (NSDAP) and the dictator of Germany from 1933 to 1945. He exploited economic unrest and nationalism to gain absolute control.

World War II

  • Invasion of Poland: His invasion of Poland in 1939 triggered the start of World War II, the deadliest conflict in human history.
  • Totalitarian Rule: He transformed Germany into a one-party state, suppressing all opposition and controlling every aspect of life.
  • Military Strategy: While initially successful in capturing much of Europe, his strategic errors led to Germany's ultimate defeat.

The Holocaust

Hitler's regime was responsible for the Holocaust, the systematic, state-sponsored persecution and murder of approximately six million Jews and millions of others by the Nazi regime and its collaborators.
