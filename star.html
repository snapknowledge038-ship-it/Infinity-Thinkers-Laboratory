Stars

What is a Star?

A star is a massive sphere of plasma held together by its own gravity, emitting intense heat and light. This immense energy is produced by a process called Nuclear Fusion occurring at the star's core.

Notable Stars

  • The Sun: The closest star to Earth. It is a medium-sized star classified as a 'Yellow Dwarf'.
  • Proxima Centauri: The nearest star to our solar system after the Sun, located about 4.24 light-years away.
  • Sirius: The brightest star visible in the night sky.

Life Cycle of a Star

Stars go through several stages from birth to death:
  1. Nebula: Stars are born within massive clouds of dust and gas in space.
  2. Main Sequence: The stable phase where a star spends most of its life (e.g., our Sun).
  3. Final Stages: Depending on its mass, a star can end its life as a Red Giant, White Dwarf, Neutron Star, or a Black Hole.

Constellations

Humans have grouped stars into various patterns in the sky, known as constellations.
  • Orion (The Hunter): Contains very bright stars like Betelgeuse and Rigel.
  • Scorpius (The Scorpion): Featuring the prominent red star Antares.
