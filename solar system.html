Solar System

What is the Solar System?

The Solar System is a gravity-bound system consisting of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. This includes 8 major planets, several dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. The planets are generally divided into two categories:
  • 1. Inner (Terrestrial) Planets: Small, rocky worlds.
  • 2. Outer (Jovian) Planets: Massive worlds made of gas and ice.

    • The Inner Planets (Terrestrial Planets)

    These planets are closest to the Sun and are characterized by solid, rocky surfaces.
  • • Mercury: The smallest planet and the closest to the Sun. It has almost no atmosphere to trap heat, leading to extreme temperature swings.
  • • Venus: Often called Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size. It is the hottest planet in our solar system because of a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat.
  • • Earth: Our home and the only planet known to harbor life. It is the only planet with liquid water on its surface.
  • • Mars: Known as the "Red Planet" because of iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It features the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons.

    • The Outer Planets (Gas & Ice Giants)

    These planets are located beyond the asteroid belt. They are massive and lack a solid surface.
  • • Jupiter: The largest planet in the solar system. It is famous for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has been raging for centuries.
  • • Saturn: Best known for its spectacular and complex ring system made of ice and dust. It is the second-largest planet.
  • • Uranus: Classified as an "Ice Giant." It has a unique tilt, rotating on its side, and appears blue-green due to methane gas.
  • • Neptune: The farthest planet from the Sun. It is a dark, cold, and incredibly windy world with supersonic winds.

    Dwarf Planets

    In the past, Pluto was considered the ninth planet. However, in 2006, it was reclassified as a Dwarf Planet. Other notable dwarf planets include Eris, Ceres, Haumea, and Makemake.
