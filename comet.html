Comet

1. What is a Comet?

A comet is a celestial body composed of ice, dust, and small rocky particles. They travel in highly elliptical orbits around the Sun. Because they originate from the cold outer reaches of the solar system, they are often nicknamed "dirty snowballs."

2. Main Parts of a Comet

  • Nucleus: The solid core made of frozen gases and dust.
  • Coma: The glowing cloud of gas and dust that forms as the ice vaporizes near the Sun.
  • Tail: Solar winds push gas and dust away from the Sun, creating a tail that can stretch for millions of kilometers.

3. Where Do Comets Come From?

  1. Kuiper Belt: A region of icy objects located just beyond Neptune.
  2. Oort Cloud: A giant spherical shell at the very edge of our solar system.

4. Famous Comets

  • Halley’s Comet: Visible from Earth every 75–76 years. It is predicted to appear next in 2061.
  • Hale-Bopp: One of the brightest comets ever, visible to the naked eye in 1997.

Fascinating Facts

  • Tail Direction: A comet's tail always points away from the Sun, regardless of its travel direction, due to solar wind.
  • Meteor Showers: When Earth passes through the dust trail left by a comet, we see a meteor shower.
