ELIZABETH I

The Virgin Queen

Daughter of Henry VIII and Anne Boleyn, Elizabeth I was the last monarch of the Tudor dynasty. She ruled England for 44 years, a period that became known as the Elizabethan Era.

The Golden Age

  • Spanish Armada: In 1588, her navy famously defeated the massive Spanish fleet, establishing England as a dominant sea power.
  • Cultural Renaissance: Her reign saw the rise of English drama, led by playwrights like William Shakespeare and Christopher Marlowe.
  • Global Exploration: She supported voyages of discovery by explorers like Sir Francis Drake and Sir Walter Raleigh.

A Powerful Legacy

Elizabeth was known for her intelligence, caution, and fiery spirit. She famously declared, "I know I have the body of a weak and feeble woman; but I have the heart and stomach of a king." She stabilized the country and laid the foundation for the British Empire.
