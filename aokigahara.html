Aokigahara Forest

Located at the base of Mount Fuji in Japan, this forest is also known as the "Sea of Trees" (Jukai). It covers an area of approximately 30 square kilometers, formed on hardened lava from a major eruption about 1,200 years ago.

Geological Features

  • Caves: There are numerous Ice Caves and Wind Caves located within the forest.
  • Sound: The porous lava rock absorbs sound, creating an eerie sense of absolute silence.
  • Magnetic Fields: Rich iron deposits in the volcanic soil can cause compasses to malfunction.

Biodiversity

  • Wildlife: Asian black bears, foxes, and various bird species inhabit the area.
  • Flora: Ancient coniferous trees and branches covered in thick moss give the forest its haunting appearance.

A Dark Reputation

  • Yurei (Ghosts): Folklore says the forest is haunted by restless spirits known as Yurei.
  • Public Attention: Known as a site for suicides, the government has placed signs urging visitors to seek help and think of their families.

Tourism and Safety

  • Many tourists visit the Narusawa Ice Cave while visiting Mount Fuji.
  • It is perfectly safe for hikers as long as they stay on officially marked designated trails.
